<section class="detail-section detail-section-results">
  <p class="section-label">Overview</p>
  <h2>The project focused on building the pipeline, not just the score.</h2>
  <div class="detail-columns">
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Context</h3>
        <div class="detail-copy">
        I wanted to analyze how tone shifted across a large public tweet history. The
                interesting part was not only the sentiment output, but also the full workflow
                required to collect, store, and process the text consistently.
        
                Public social data tends to look simple from far away, but it becomes a data
                engineering problem as soon as the collection and storage steps matter.
      </div>

    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Approach</h3>
        <div class="detail-copy">
        I collected historical tweets, stored them in PostgreSQL on AWS, and used VADER
                sentiment scoring through NLTK to build a time-based view of emotional change.
        
                The project was useful because it linked text analysis to a concrete storage and
                retrieval layer instead of treating the scoring step in isolation.
      </div>

    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="detail-section detail-section-takeaways">
  <p class="section-label">Workflow</p>
  <h2>Collection, storage, and scoring needed to work as one system.</h2>
  <div class="detail-stack">
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Collection</h3>
        <div class="detail-copy">
        Historical tweets were gathered and normalized so they could be processed in a
                consistent format.
      </div>

    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Storage</h3>
        <div class="detail-copy">
        PostgreSQL on AWS provided a practical way to store and query the tweet history
                once the dataset moved beyond a simple local experiment.
      </div>

    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Sentiment scoring</h3>
        <div class="detail-copy">
        VADER sentiment scoring was applied to create a simple time-based signal of how
                the tone shifted over the corpus.
      </div>

    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="detail-section">
  <p class="section-label">Takeaways</p>
  <h2>Text analysis becomes more credible when the data workflow is explicit.</h2>
  <div class="detail-stack">
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>What mattered</h3>
        <div class="detail-copy">
        The durable part of the project was the end-to-end workflow from collection to
                storage to scoring, not the sentiment number by itself.
      </div>

    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Why it was useful</h3>
        <div class="detail-copy">
