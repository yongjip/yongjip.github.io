  <DetailColumns>
    <DetailItem title="Context">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>I wanted to analyze how tone shifted across a large public tweet history. The interesting part was not only the sentiment output, but also the full workflow required to collect, store, and process the text consistently.</p>
        <p>Public social data tends to look simple from far away, but it becomes a data engineering problem as soon as the collection and storage steps matter.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Approach">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>I collected historical tweets, stored them in PostgreSQL on AWS, and used VADER sentiment scoring through NLTK to build a time-based view of emotional change.</p>
        <p>The project was useful because it linked text analysis to a concrete storage and retrieval layer instead of treating the scoring step in isolation.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
  </DetailColumns>
</DetailSection>

        <p>Historical tweets were gathered and normalized so they could be processed in a consistent format.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Storage">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>PostgreSQL on AWS provided a practical way to store and query the tweet history once the dataset moved beyond a simple local experiment.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Sentiment scoring">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>VADER sentiment scoring was applied to create a simple time-based signal of how the tone shifted over the corpus.</p>

    </DetailItem>
  </DetailStack>
