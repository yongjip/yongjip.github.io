<DetailSection
  label="Overview"
  title="The same inventory questions were being answered too many times by hand."
  class="detail-section-results"
>
  <DetailColumns>
    <DetailItem title="Context">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>In an earlier retail operations role, planners and business stakeholders repeatedly needed the same kinds of visibility: inventory with no sellable path, stock risk caused by process gaps, unusual ordering patterns, and category views that were difficult to assemble quickly from raw tables.</p>
        <p>The problem was not a lack of data. It was that the data was still too expensive to turn into a usable operating view every time the question came up.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Approach">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>I built a set of recurring reporting workflows and self-serve dashboards that turned those repeated questions into scheduled outputs. The goal was to reduce repeated manual follow-up, not to create a large reporting platform.</p>
        <p>The useful design choice was keeping each output simple: a clear summary for fast scanning and enough detail behind it for follow-up action.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
  </DetailColumns>
</DetailSection>

<DetailSection
  label="Workflow"
  title="Scheduled accumulation and readable outputs mattered more than report volume."
  class="detail-section-takeaways"
>
  <DetailStack>
    <DetailItem title="Recurring refresh">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>Data was accumulated on a daily or periodic cadence so reports reflected recent operational state without requiring a fresh analyst pass each time.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Summary first">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>Each output started with a compact summary view so category leads and planners could tell quickly whether attention was needed.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Actionable detail">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>Reports also included the raw or near-raw detail needed for follow-up, so users could move from signal to action without asking for a second analysis.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Self-serve dashboards">
      <DetailCopy>
        <p>Where recurring monitoring was more useful than a static report, I built dashboard views so stakeholders could inspect current conditions directly.</p>
      </DetailCopy>
    </DetailItem>
  </DetailStack>
</DetailSection>
