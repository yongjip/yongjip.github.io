<section class="detail-section detail-section-results">
  <p class="section-label">Overview</p>
  <h2>Text-to-SQL only became useful after the data model was made explicit.</h2>
  <div class="detail-columns">
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Context</h3>
      <p>
        Ad-hoc analytics requests were consuming too much analyst time. In practice, the
        slow part was not SQL syntax. It was finding the right tables, understanding what
        columns meant, mapping business logic correctly, and figuring out join patterns
        that could actually be trusted.
      </p>
      <p>
        That made even routine questions expensive. Complex requests often took 4 to 10
        hours, which limited how many requests the team could realistically support each
        month.
      </p>
    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Approach</h3>
      <p>
        I built a structured metadata layer in YAML that captured how analysts actually
        reason about the data model: table definitions, column semantics, keys,
        partitioning, join patterns, and business terminology.
      </p>
      <p>
        The workflow then used AI coding agents to generate SQL, execute it in Athena,
        and validate results before anything was handed back. The leverage came from
        structuring context well enough that the system could reuse it repeatedly.
      </p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="detail-section detail-section-takeaways">
  <p class="section-label">Workflow</p>
  <h2>A practical validation loop around AI-generated SQL</h2>
  <p class="detail-intro">
    The workflow narrowed context first, then generated, executed, and checked SQL before
    any result was handed back to a human.
  </p>
  <div class="detail-stack">
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Scoped context</h3>
      <p>Tables were grouped by domain and narrowed through index files so the agent only saw the relevant slice of metadata.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>SQL generation</h3>
      <p>AI coding agents used the structured metadata to generate and revise SQL based on the actual business question.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Execution and QA</h3>
      <p>The workflow ran queries in Athena, checked execution success, and validated results before handing anything back to a human.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>Reusable knowledge</h3>
      <p>The resulting structure could be updated and reused so later requests started from a better base instead of from scratch.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

  <div class="detail-stack">
    <article class="detail-item">
      <h3>What mattered</h3>
