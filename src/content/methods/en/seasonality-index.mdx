<DetailSection label="Overview" title="Inventory planning improves when seasonality is measured below the category level.">
  <DetailColumns>
    <DetailItem title="Problem">
      <p>In inventory planning, it is tempting to assign one seasonal pattern to an entire category. In practice, that can be too coarse. Products in the same category may follow different demand curves across the year.</p>
      <p>That creates a planning problem: category-level seasonality is simple, but product-level behavior is often what actually drives inventory risk.</p>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Approach">
      <p>The method starts by calculating a seasonality index at the SKU level rather than relying only on category averages. Products can then be grouped into seasonal buckets based on similar patterns.</p>
      <p>The point is not to create the most complex model. It is to keep the planning logic simple while making the seasonal signal more realistic.</p>
    </DetailItem>
  </DetailColumns>
</DetailSection>

<DetailSection label="Method" title="A structured way to turn noisy product behavior into usable planning signals.">
  <DetailStack>
    <DetailItem title="Product-level SI">
      <p>Estimate seasonality at the product level first so each SKU can reflect its own demand pattern across time.</p>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Season buckets">
      <p>Group products into a smaller set of seasonal buckets so the result stays usable for business planning instead of becoming too fragmented.</p>
    </DetailItem>
    <DetailItem title="Planning linkage">
      <p>Map those patterns back into category-level planning logic so teams can use a cleaner operational framework without ignoring product differences.</p>
    </DetailItem>
  </DetailStack>
</DetailSection>
